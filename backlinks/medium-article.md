The Gold Coast 1990s Pool Wave Is Hitting the Resurface Window — Here's What That Actually Costs in 2026

## Body

If you bought a Gold Coast home in the last decade with a 1990s or early-2000s pool out the back, here's a number that should focus your attention: the original pebble interior was built to last 12 to 20 years. Most of those original pebble surfaces are now 25 to 35 years old.

Your pool surface is on borrowed time. The QBCC Pool Safety Inspector who turns up at your sale settlement will spot it in 30 seconds. So will the buyer's pre-purchase report.

The good news: resurfacing is actually one of the highest-leverage spends in pool ownership. Get it right and the pool looks new for another 15-25 years. Get it wrong — or get bullied into "you may as well replace the whole thing" by a builder who quoted concrete-pool prices — and you're $80K poorer for no good reason.

### How to tell your pool needs resurfacing now

Walk down the steps and run your hand along the floor. If it feels like rough sandpaper that scrapes your feet, the pebble aggregate matrix is breaking down. That's not a one-off — it's structural. Pebble surfaces hold together because the cement matrix bonds the aggregate. Once the cement starts giving way, the aggregate pops out, and the whole surface chases the failure outward.

Other tells:

- **Aggregate "plucking"** — small pin-holes where individual stones have popped out, leaving a pock-marked surface.
- **Rust streaks that won't acid-wash off** — these are from rebar corrosion bleeding through micro-cracks. Your shell is no longer waterproof at those points.
- **Dark grey or beige patches** where pH chemistry has fried the surface (usually from chronic incorrect chlorinator setpoints).
- **Chemistry that won't hold** — you add chlorine, it's gone in two days, because the porous failing surface is absorbing it.
- **Visible rebar** — URGENT. The concrete shell is spalling. Resurface NOW or face full structural failure.

### The Gold Coast salt accelerator

Most Gold Coast pools run salt chlorinators rather than handling liquid chlorine in 35°C / 80% humidity. Salt is gentler on swimmers but harsher on the pool. Salt-chlorinator water sits at the lower end of the pebble lifespan range — 12-15 years rather than 18-20.

That's why a Gold Coast 1990s pool with original pebble is right in the second-cycle resurface window NOW, while an equivalent inland Brisbane pool might still have another five years.

### What resurfacing actually costs in 2026

Here are the honest 2026 Gold Coast price bands for an 8m x 4m family pool:

- **Pebble-on-pebble replacement (Pebblecrete, Quartzon Original, Diamond Brite).** $9,000-$14,000. Direct like-for-like. 12-18 year next-life.
- **Quartz upgrade (Quartzon Quartz, Diamond Brite Quartz).** $12,000-$18,000. Smoother and more durable than pebble. 18-25 year next-life. Less heel-rough on bare feet. The 2026 standard upgrade choice.
- **Full mosaic glass or ceramic tile.** $25,000-$50,000. Tile the entire interior with 25mm mosaic. 30+ year lifespan if grout maintained. Premium look. Common on Burleigh Hill and canal-estate rebuilds.
- **Mineral / composite surface.** $18,000-$28,000. Newer Bisazza-style hybrid finish, between quartz and tile.

Add a coping replacement at the same time — almost everyone does — for another $3K-$8K. Add new paving around the pool surround for $5K-$20K. Add a glass-fence upgrade to bring the barrier up to current QDC MP 3.4 / AS 1926.1 compliance for $4K-$12K.

A typical full Gold Coast pool refresh — resurface + coping + glass fence + new paving — lands at $30K-$60K and the pool looks better than new. That's vs $80K-$140K for a complete pool replacement.

### When "rebuild" really is the right call

About 5-10% of pre-quote assessments end with us recommending replacement rather than resurface. Three situations trigger this:

1. **Rebar is going.** Severe rust bleed means the steel cage is corroded. Bonding a new surface over a deteriorating substrate is throwing money at a problem that comes back in three years.
2. **Major structural cracks.** Hairline cracks under 1mm are cosmetic. Anything over 1mm or actively leaking water needs structural investigation. Sometimes the underlying issue is hydrostatic uplift (water table) or settlement — symptoms of which won't be fixed by resurface.
3. **Three resurfaces deep already.** Each resurface adds 15-25mm of substrate. After three, the pool dimensions are noticeably altered and the load-path is compromised.

### The QBCC licence check (don't skip this)

Queensland licenses swimming pool construction under a specific QBCC class — Class 1 Builder with the swimming pool contractor endorsement. A general builder without that endorsement **cannot lawfully build, resurface or substantially renovate a pool** in QLD.

Plenty of cowboys quote pool work without the right ticket. The building certifier checks the licence number on the Form 17 compliance certificate, and the unsuspecting owner discovers the problem after settlement when the building insurance won't certify. Always confirm the QBCC number is on the quote, not just an ABN.

### What to ask before signing a resurface quote

1. **What's the QBCC licence number?** Not just an ABN.
2. **Is the existing shell structurally sound?** If they haven't checked, they're guessing.
3. **Pebble, quartz or tile?** Each has different lifespan. Get a specific recommendation with reasoning.
4. **What's the warranty on the new surface?** 5-7 years is standard.
5. **Does the quote include coping, fence compliance, equipment audit?** Or is it interior-only and the rest extra?
